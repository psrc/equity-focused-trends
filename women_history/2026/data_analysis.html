median person’s earnings by gender and race and ethnicity

  • only include full-time workers working more than 35hr/wk
  • the results show that median earnings of male full-time workers are higher than female full-time workers in some racial groups, including
    • Asian
    • Hispanic or Latino
    • Some Other Race
    • Two or More Races
    • White
  • while median earnings are statistically equal in other racial groups
    • American Indian or Alaskan Native
    • Black or African American
    • Native Hawaiian and Other Pacific Islander
Note

categories that female and male full-time workers have statistically equal median earnings (moe bars overlapping) due to low sample size:

  • American Indian or Alaskan Native
  • Black or African American
  • Native Hawaiian and Other Pacific Islander

trip purpose by gender

  • this chart shows the share of trips by trip purpose for female and male
  • the results show that men makes more Work/School trips than women across all survey years, but the gap is narrowing over time
  • in 2017/2019, women makes more Errand/Other, Escort trips than men
  • in 2021, women makes more Shop trips than men
Note

list of trip purposes that have statistically significant differences in share of trips between female and male:

  • 2017/2019:
    • f>m: Errand/Other, Escort
    • m>f: Work/School
  • 2021:
    • f>m: Shop
    • m>f: Work/School
  • 2023:
    • f>m:
    • m>f: Work/School

mode share by gender

  • the chart shows the trip mode shares by gender
  • the results show that women drive more than men in 2017/2019 and 2023
  • men bike (in all years), walk (in 2017/2019 and 2023) and use transit (in 2023) more than women
  • may pose the question: does this imply that women may have greater safety concerns when using these travel modes?
Note

list of modes that have statistically significant differences in mode share between female and male:

  • 2017/2019:
    • f>m: Drive
    • m>f: Bike/Micromobility, Walk
  • 2021:
    • f>m:
    • m>f: Bike/Micromobility
  • 2023:
    • f>m: Drive
    • m>f: Bike/Micromobility, Transit, Walk
